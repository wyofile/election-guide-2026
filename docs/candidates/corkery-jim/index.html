On the Issues

The answers below were solicited from candidates via a written questionnaire created by WyoFile reporters and editors in June 2026. Responses are presented exactly as submitted, without fact-checking, wordsmithing or editing for grammar, punctuation or spelling errors.

Legislative candidates were invited to respond to the questionnaire several times by email and by phone. Out of fairness to the candidates who met the deadline, WyoFile will not add responses after the guide's publication.

Where do you live currently? How long have you lived there? How long have you lived in Wyoming? Where were you born?

I currently live in Casper, WY. I have lived here nearly 6 years. I was born in Truckee, CA which is near Lake Tahoe.

What age will you be on Election Day, Nov. 3, 2026?

60

Please tell the voters about yourself including your background and qualifications.

I am a husband, father, business person, and community volunteer. While in business, I managed various roles from account servicing, compliance, and delivering incentive payments to internal and external business partners. In this role, I worked with auditing staff from all levels of government. I also managed staff in each of the roles I held.

I have also been involved in various community service efforts. I was the head of a mens group within my church, which on an annual basis put on a fundraiser for a local food bank and participated in a program to provide winter coats to children in need. I am volunteer National Ski Patroller up at Casper Mountain. Our role is to maintain safety and provide basic first aid and safe transport of injured skiers to next level care.

What are the biggest challenges and opportunities facing Wyoming today? As a legislator, how would you address them?

The biggest challenges for Wyoming is reliance on mineral revenues which tend to fluctuate, the need to diversify economic opportunities, and spending at all levels of government. I would start by making sure our regulatory statutes are not contributing to these challenges and we are promoting policy to encourage opportunities. We need to closely watch spending to make sure essential services are the first to be funded without increasing taxes to support them. And challenge government to look at different ways to provide services more efficiently and cost effective - ie. Internet Delivery, New Technologies. I want to demonstrate to the public that government provides high level of service in the most efficient possible way.

We asked WyoFile readers to rank issues that are important to them, and healthcare costs and access topped the list. What can the Legislature do to make healthcare more affordable and accessible to Wyomingites?

The first effort is looking at the ability to deliver services. I believe Telehealth is one option that can assist and we want to make sure there are no impediments in the use of this option. Another is looking into options to incentivize traveling nurses and other specialty providers that will encourage them to come provide services in Wyoming.

Finally, we need to look at underserved areas and find ways to encourage healthcare delivery - perhaps tax incentives, targeting loan forgiveness, and promotion of rural medical training tracks.

Do you support efforts to sell or transfer public land in Wyoming? How do you plan to ensure that state and federal land is managed in the best interest of the people of Wyoming?

I don't support efforts to sell or transfer public land in Wyoming.

No two Wyoming counties are the same when it comes to how they generate revenue. As a legislator, how would you approach the public's desire for property tax relief with local revenue needs?

I support the most recent legislative efforts of property tax relief as they have attempted to balance relief to the tax payer while providing funding for local revenue needs. The legislature has offset cuts with backfill of other revenue. I would look to make these measures more permanent which will help the state and local governments plan growth within a consistent framework.

What role, if any, should the Wyoming Legislature play in economic development?

I think the Legislature should create an economic, regulatory and educational environment that would incentivize business to develop in Wyoming and encourage existing business to strongly consider moving operations and jobs to our state. And I think there are many opportunities to do so. I think the current method used has not shown solid results and it appears all involved realize change in this process is necessary.

Explain your values when it comes to campaign donations. Does Wyoming's system for monitoring campaign donations and expenditures need reform?

I believe in complete transparency when it comes to campaign donations. I think the current Wyoming system works well with the exception of the timing. I think the timing of disclosure be shown earlier than a week before in-person voting.
